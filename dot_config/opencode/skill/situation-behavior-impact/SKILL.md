# Situation-Behavior-Impact (SBI)

**Category:** Communication
**Source:** Center for Creative Leadership (CCL)

Use this when giving feedback — especially corrective feedback — to avoid judgment, generalization, and defensiveness. Makes feedback concrete and discussion-oriented rather than evaluative.

## The Framework

### 1. Situation
Describe the specific context where the behavior occurred. Grounds the feedback in a real, shared moment.

> "In yesterday's sprint review meeting..."
> "When you submitted the PR last Thursday..."

### 2. Behavior
Describe what the person *did* or *said* — observable actions only. No interpretation, no labels, no character judgments.

> "...you didn't share the test results before proposing the merge..."
> "...you interrupted the client twice while they were explaining the requirements..."

### 3. Impact
Explain what effect their behavior had — on you, on others, or on the work.

> "...which made me uncertain whether the change was safe to ship."
> "...which left the client looking frustrated and may have affected their confidence in us."

### + Intent (optional but powerful)
Ask what their intention was. This surfaces the gap between intent and impact, and opens dialogue.

> "What was your thinking there?"
> "Was there a reason you didn't include the test results?"

## Agent Workflow

When helping someone prepare feedback:

1. Ask: Who is the feedback for, and what did they do?
2. Help nail the **Situation** — specific time, place, event.
3. Refine the **Behavior** — strip out interpretation. "Was unhelpful" → "said X." "Was disruptive" → "interrupted twice."
4. Clarify the **Impact** — first-person where possible ("I felt...", "the team had to...").
5. Draft the full SBI message.
6. Optionally: draft an intent question to open the follow-up conversation.

## Example

**Draft (with judgment):** "Alex is really not a team player. He never thinks about how his decisions affect others."

**SBI version:**
- **Situation:** "In Monday's architecture discussion..."
- **Behavior:** "...you made the decision to switch to a new library and merged the PR without flagging it in the channel first..."
- **Impact:** "...which meant two other engineers had to stop mid-task to deal with unexpected conflicts in their branches. I felt blindsided."
- **Intent question:** "Was there a reason you moved quickly on that one?"

## Tips

- **Positive feedback works the same way.** SBI prevents vague praise ("great job!") and makes recognition meaningful.
- **One behavior per session.** Stacking multiple behaviors dilutes each one and feels like an attack.
- **Impact > intent.** Even if intent was good, the impact is real and worth discussing.
