# Inversion

**Category:** Problem Solving
**Source:** Charlie Munger, James Clear, Wes O'Haire

Use this when you're only thinking about ideal outcomes and need to stress-test your thinking. Inversion forces you to consider what could go wrong — and often reveals risks and requirements that forward thinking misses.

## The Core Idea

Instead of asking *"How do I succeed?"*, ask *"What would guarantee failure?"*

Then work backward: if those are the conditions for failure, how do you avoid or prevent each one?

## Two Applications

### Application 1: Evaluate a decision or solution
1. Ask: *"What would be the worst possible decision or solution here?"*
2. Ask: *"Why would it be bad? What specifically would go wrong?"*
3. Look at your current plan: does it avoid those failure modes? What adjustments are needed?

### Application 2: Pre-mortem (project/team setting)
Imagine it's 6 months from now. The project has failed. Gather the team and ask:
- *"What went wrong?"*
- *"What mistakes did we make?"*
- *"Why did this fail?"*

Document all answers. Use them to identify risks to mitigate before the project starts.

## Trigger Question
Ask yourself: *"Am I only thinking about ideal scenarios?"*

If yes, apply inversion before committing to a plan.

## Agent Workflow

When helping evaluate a plan, decision, or design:

1. State what's being evaluated.
2. Generate 5–10 specific failure modes: *"This fails if..."*
3. For each failure mode, assess: Is this already mitigated? If not, what would mitigate it?
4. Identify the top 2–3 highest-risk unmitigated failure modes.
5. Recommend changes to the plan to address them.

## Example

**Plan:** Launch a new pricing tier to increase revenue.

**Inversion — what would make this fail?**
- Existing customers feel they're being downgraded and churn
- New tier is too close to existing tiers — customers don't see the value distinction
- Sales team doesn't understand the new tiers and gives inconsistent messaging
- New tier cannibalizes the tier above it without expanding total revenue
- Launch timing conflicts with a competitor announcement — message gets buried
- Pricing page becomes confusing — conversion drops across all tiers

**Mitigations to add to the plan:**
- Grandfathering policy for existing customers (addresses churn risk)
- Differentiation testing with 10 customers before launch (addresses blur risk)
- Sales enablement session with Q&A (addresses messaging risk)
- Revenue modeling by tier to check cannibalization (addresses revenue risk)

## Tips

- Inversion doesn't mean pessimism. It's a tool for thoroughness.
- The best use is *before* committing to a plan, not after something goes wrong.
- Combine with Second-Order Thinking for a fuller picture of consequences.
